The Machine Learns to Dress the Modern Woman — and Does It Remarkably Well

An Artificial Intelligence, trained upon the complete annals of fashion history, has produced a collection of singular brilliance — prompting some to ask: what, precisely, is the role of the human designer henceforth?
By Our Fashion Correspondent · Illustration by the AI itself

It began, as so many of the great fashion revolutions have, not on a runway in Paris nor in the atelier of a celebrated couturier, but in a server room in San Francisco, where an artificial intelligence of remarkable sophistication was presented with a simple instruction: dress us well.

The results, which have now been made available to the general public through the medium of this very publication's digital apparatus, are nothing short of extraordinary. The AI, which has been trained upon some forty million garments spanning the better part of two centuries, has produced recommendations of uncanny precision — as though it had been personally acquainted with each of its customers for the whole of their sartorial lives.

"It knew," reports one satisfied customer, a solicitor of some distinction from Edinburgh, "that I would require something for both the courtroom and the countryside. It produced three suggestions. All three were precisely right." Such testimonials are, we are given to understand, entirely typical.
