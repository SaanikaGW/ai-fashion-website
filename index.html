The Process

From anything
to something

The idea was simple: an AI fashion website. But "AI fashion website" is a blank canvas — a thousand things at once. So I started by making it many things, deliberately. Phase one was chaos. Phase two was instinct. Phase three was commitment.

01
Phase One

Going Wide

The goal here wasn't quality — it was range. I gave myself permission to go anywhere: different audiences, different eras, different emotional registers. Minimalism. Maximalism. Nostalgia. Darkness. Each one a different answer to the same question: what does AI fashion feel like?

v01 → v10
What I was testing: Could any of these aesthetics feel authentic to both fashion and AI? Or do they feel forced — like a costume instead of an identity? At this stage I wasn't editing. I was collecting evidence.

02
Phase Two

Narrowing Down

After v10, some things were resonating and others weren't. The dark, type-forward aesthetics felt most true to what an AI fashion tool might actually be — intelligent, intentional, not trying too hard. I kept pushing in adjacent directions: mystical, brutalist, noir, glass, street. Each one asking: is this it?

v11 → v19
What I was noticing: Strong typographic hierarchy and editorial restraint kept showing up in the versions that felt right. The playful ones (Y2K, vaporwave) were fun to build but felt like costumes. The dark, considered ones felt like something I'd actually use.

03
Phase Three

Converging

By now the signal was clear: strong typography, earned restraint, and an honest relationship with technology. I wasn't running from the fact that this was an AI tool — I was leaning into it. v20–v24 refined that convergence. v25 committed to it fully.

v20 → v25
The pattern: Every version I kept returning to had one thing in common — it didn't pretend AI fashion was magical. It treated the user as intelligent. The best designs were honest about what they were: a precise, type-forward, intelligent tool. That's v25.

FINAL CHOICE · v25

FASHION.SH
Terminal Fashion

The command-line aesthetic forces honesty: no pretty images to hide behind, just the AI doing its job precisely. It's an inversion — the least "fashionable" interface for a fashion tool — and that contrast is the point. It treats users as intelligent. It admits what it is.
